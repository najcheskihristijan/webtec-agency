You dropped your tablet. The screen is cracked. A repair shop quotes $200. A new tablet costs $350.

What do you do?

Here's the decision framework that actually works.

## The 50% Rule

**If the repair costs more than 50% of a replacement, buy new.**

Example:
- Repair: $200
- New tablet: $350
- Percentage: 57%
- **Decision: Replace.**

## But Wait—Factor in Age

A 1-year-old tablet? Repair it.  
A 5-year-old tablet? Replace it.

**Why?** Because even if you fix the screen, the battery, processor, and software are still 5 years old. You're one failure away from another expensive repair.

## The Exceptions

### Exception 1: High-End Devices
If you own an iPad Pro ($1,000+), the 50% rule shifts to 70%. A $300 screen repair on a $1,200 device is worth it.

### Exception 2: Sentimental Value
If it's your kid's first tablet with all their drawings saved locally, pay the $200.

### Exception 3: Environmental Impact
Repairing is always greener than replacing. If sustainability matters to you, repair even if it's slightly more expensive.

## Common Repairs: Worth It or Not?

| Issue | Typical Cost | Worth It? |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| Cracked screen | $150-$300 | Only if <2 years old |
| Dead battery | $80-$150 | Yes (if <3 years old) |
| Broken charging port | $60-$100 | Yes |
| Water damage | $200-$400 | No (too unpredictable) |
| Slow performance | $0 (factory reset) | Always try this first |

## The "Slow Tablet" Trap

Before you spend a dime, try this:
1.  **Factory Reset:** Wipes all bloat.
2.  **Update OS:** Sometimes fixes bugs.
3.  **Replace the Battery:** Often the real culprit.

A $100 battery replacement can make a 4-year-old tablet feel new.

## Conclusion

**Repair if:**
- Device is <2 years old
- Repair costs <50% of replacement
- It's a high-end model

**Replace if:**
- Device is >4 years old
- Repair costs >50% of replacement
- Multiple things are broken

When in doubt, get a second opinion. Many repair shops will diagnose for free.
