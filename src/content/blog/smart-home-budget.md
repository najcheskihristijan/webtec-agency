Smart homes are marketed as luxury tech. But the real value isn't convenience—it's **cost savings**.

Here's how to build an energy-efficient smart home for less than the cost of one month's electric bill.

## The $100 Smart Home Starter Kit

### 1. Smart Plugs with Energy Monitoring ($40)
**Buy:** TP-Link Kasa KP125M (4-pack)

These plugs turn any "dumb" appliance into a smart one. More importantly, they show you exactly how much power each device is using.

**The Strategy:**
1.  Plug in your biggest energy hogs (space heater, window AC, gaming PC).
2.  Check the app to see real-time wattage.
3.  Set schedules to turn them off automatically.

**Real-World Savings:** We cut $15/month by auto-shutting off a space heater that was running 24/7 in an empty room.

### 2. Smart Thermostat ($80)
**Buy:** Amazon Smart Thermostat

This is the single highest-ROI smart home device. It learns your schedule and automatically adjusts heating/cooling when you're away.

**Installation:** DIY in 30 minutes (YouTube has guides for every HVAC system).

**Real-World Savings:** Average of $50/year on heating and cooling.

### 3. Smart LED Bulbs ($30)
**Buy:** Wyze Bulb Color (4-pack)

LEDs already use 75% less energy than incandescent bulbs. Smart LEDs add scheduling and motion detection.

**The Strategy:**
- Set outdoor lights to turn off at sunrise.
- Dim bedroom lights automatically at 9 PM.
- Turn off all lights when you leave (via geofencing).

**Real-World Savings:** $8/month by eliminating "lights left on overnight" waste.

## Bonus: Free Energy Savers

These cost $0 and work immediately:
1.  **Seal Air Leaks:** Use caulk around windows ($5 at Home Depot). This can save $100/year.
2.  **Change HVAC Filters:** A dirty filter makes your system work 15% harder.
3.  **Use Window Coverings:** Close curtains in summer, open them in winter.

## Conclusion

You don't need a Nest Hub, smart blinds, or a $400 Ecobee to save money. Three devices—smart plugs, a thermostat, and LED bulbs—will deliver 80% of the savings for 10% of the cost.
