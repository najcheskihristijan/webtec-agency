Surf fishing has been completely revolutionized by drone technology. Getting your bait out 500 meters past the breakers used to require a boat or a kayak. Now, you can deploy a drone from the beach, drop your bait with pinpoint precision, and scout for schools of tuna or GTs from the air.

But the ocean is a hostile environment for electronics. Salt spray, mist, and the risk of a water landing mean that a standard camera drone simply won't cut it. You need a **waterproof fishing drone**.

In this guide, we've ranked the 5 best waterproof drones for fishing in 2026, from heavy-lifting beasts to versatile all-rounders.

## 1. SwellPro SplashDrone 4
**The "Swiss Army Knife" of Fishing Drones**

The **SwellPro SplashDrone 4** is widely considered the gold standard for waterproof drones. It bridges the gap between a high-end camera drone and a rugged fishing tool. It’s fully IP67 waterproof, meaning it can land on water and take off again, which is a massive stress-saver when flying over the ocean.

*   **Key Features:** 4K Camera with 3-axis gimbal, IP67 Waterproof, Slide-in battery.
*   **Max Payload:** 2kg (4.4 lbs)
*   **Flight Time:** Up to 30 mins (without payload). *Tip: Keep it charged in the field with a [Solar Power Bank](/blog/solar-power-banks-review).*

**Why we love it:** It supports various modules. You can swap the camera for a simple bait-release mechanism or use the PL-1 payload release with a 4K camera to film the strike.

## 2. SwellPro Fisherman FD3
**The Heavy-Duty Workhorse**

If you don't care about 4K video and just want to drop a massive bait for sharks or marlin, the **Fisherman FD3** is your beast. It strips away the complex filming features to focus on raw power and durability.

*   **Key Features:** Simplified remote, extreme wind resistance, IP67 Waterproof.
*   **Max Payload:** 3.5kg (7.7 lbs) !
*   **Flight Time:** Up to 27 mins

**Why we love it:** The lifting capacity. 3.5kg is enough for a large bonito bait plus a heavy sinker. It’s built like a tank and designed purely for the job of delivering bait.

## 2026 Buyer's Guide: What to Look For

When buying a fishing drone, ignore the "follow-me" modes and obstacle avoidance. Focus on these three metrics:

1.  **IP Rating (Waterproofing):** Look for **IP67**. This means it can be submerged up to 1 meter for 30 minutes. "Water-resistant" is okay for spray, but "Waterproof" saves your investment if you crash.
2.  **Payload Capacity:** Calculate your rig weight (Sinker + Bait). A frozen pilchard + 4oz sinker weighs around 250g-300g. A live mullet + 8oz sinker can weigh over 1kg. Buy a drone that handles *more* than you plan to carry.
3.  **Wind Resistance:** The beach is always windy. Drones like the FD3 typically handle high winds (Level 7) better than lighter camera drones.
