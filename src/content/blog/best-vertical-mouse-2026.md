I used a traditional mouse for 15 years. Then I developed RSI (repetitive strain injury) in my right wrist.

Switching to a vertical mouse eliminated the pain in 2 weeks. Here's why.

## The Problem: Pronation

When you use a traditional mouse, your forearm twists so your palm faces down. This is called **pronation**, and it puts constant stress on your tendons.

Do this 8 hours a day, and you're headed for:
- Carpal tunnel syndrome
- Tennis elbow
- Chronic wrist pain

## The Solution: Vertical Mice

A vertical mouse rotates your hand 90 degrees into a "handshake" position. This eliminates pronation and aligns your wrist naturally.

## The Learning Curve

**Week 1:** Feels weird. You'll be slower.
**Week 2:** Muscle memory kicks in. Speed returns to normal.
**Week 3:** You'll never go back.

Give it 2 weeks before you judge.

## Who Should Use a Vertical Mouse?

**Yes, if:**
- You have wrist pain
- You use a mouse 4+ hours/day
- You've been diagnosed with RSI or carpal tunnel

**No, if:**
- You're a competitive gamer (traditional mice are faster for gaming)
- You have no wrist pain and use a mouse <2 hours/day
