Streaming 4K video requires serious bandwidth and hardware. But you don't need to spend thousands.

Here's the budget setup that actually works.

## The Bottlenecks

4K streaming fails for 3 reasons:
1.  **Slow Internet:** You need 25 Mbps minimum.
2.  **Weak Hardware:** Your device can't decode 4K.
3.  **Bad Wi-Fi:** Your router is from 2015.

Fix these three, and you're golden.

## The $500 Budget Breakdown

### 1. Streaming Device ($50)
**Buy:** Roku Ultra or Amazon Fire TV Stick 4K Max

Both support 4K HDR and have fast processors. The Roku is slightly better for non-Amazon content.

**Don't Buy:** A $1,000 gaming PC just for streaming. Overkill.

### 2. Router Upgrade ($120)
**Buy:** TP-Link Archer AX55 (Wi-Fi 6)

If your router is >3 years old, it's your bottleneck. Wi-Fi 6 routers handle 4K streams without buffering.

**Setup Tip:** Place the router in a central location, not in a closet.

### 3. Ethernet Cable ($15)
**Buy:** Cat 6 Ethernet Cable (25ft)

Wi-Fi is convenient, but Ethernet is *always* faster and more stable. Run a cable from your router to your streaming device if possible.

### 4. HDMI 2.1 Cable ($20)
**Buy:** Certified Ultra High Speed HDMI Cable

Your old HDMI cable might not support 4K@60fps. Spend $20 on a certified cable.

### 5. Internet Upgrade ($0-$50/month)
**Check:** Your current plan's speed.

For 4K streaming, you need:
- **1 stream:** 25 Mbps
- **2 streams:** 50 Mbps
- **4 streams:** 100 Mbps

If you're below this, upgrade your plan.

## Total Cost: ~$200 (one-time) + potential internet upgrade

## Advanced: Reduce Buffering

If you still experience lag:
1.  **Close Background Apps:** Your phone updating apps eats bandwidth.
2.  **Use 5GHz Wi-Fi:** It's faster than 2.4GHz (but shorter range).
3.  **Enable QoS:** In your router settings, prioritize streaming traffic.

## Conclusion

You don't need a $2,000 gaming rig to stream 4K. A $50 Roku Ultra + a $120 Wi-Fi 6 router + Ethernet = flawless 4K streaming.

Spend your money on the **router** and **internet plan**, not the streaming device.
