## 1. Aiper Scuba S1 - Best Overall
**$498 | Cordless | Walls + Floor**

The Scuba S1 punches way above its weight class. It's cordless (no tangled cables!), climbs walls, and has exceptional suction power.

*   **Test Results:** Cleaned our 20x40ft pool in 2.5 hours. Picked up leaves, dirt, and even fine sand.
*   **Battery Life:** 150 minutes per charge.
*   **Drawback:** The filter basket fills up quickly with heavy debris.

**Verdict:** If you have one pool to clean and $500 to spend, buy this.

## 2. Dolphin Nautilus Pool Up - Best for Walls
**$449 | Corded | SmartNav Technology**

Dolphin's reputation for quality is well-earned. The Nautilus Pool Up is specifically designed for small in-ground pools and excels at wall climbing.

*   **Test Results:** Consistently climbed to the waterline without getting stuck.
*   **SmartNav:** Maps the pool to avoid redundant cleaning paths.
*   **Drawback:** Corded design means you need to untangle it occasionally.

**Verdict:** Best for pools with heavy algae buildup on walls.

## 3. Aiper Seagull Pro - Best Cordless
**$479 | Cordless | WavePath Navigation**

If you hate cables, the Seagull Pro is your answer. It uses "WavePath Navigation" to systematically cover the entire pool floor and walls.

*   **Test Results:** Excellent floor cleaning. Wall performance was good but not perfect.
*   **Runtime:** 150 minutes.
*   **Drawback:** Struggles with very large leaves.

**Verdict:** Great for pools up to 1,600 sq ft.

## 4. Dolphin E10 - Best for Above-Ground
**$500 | Corded | Lightweight**

At just 14 pounds, the E10 is purpose-built for above-ground pools. It's easy to lift in and out.

*   **Test Results:** Spotless floor cleaning. No wall climbing (by design).
*   **Durability:** Solidly built. We expect 3+ years of use.
*   **Drawback:** No scheduling feature.

**Verdict:** Perfect for Intex-style pools.

## 5. Aiper Scuba SE - Best Ultra-Budget
**$140 | Cordless | Floor Only**

If $500 is still too much, the Scuba SE gets the job done for $140. It's floor-only, but it works.

*   **Test Results:** Adequate for small pools with light debris.
*   **Battery:** 90 minutes.
*   **Drawback:** No wall climbing. Small filter.

**Verdict:** Better than a manual vacuum, barely.

---

## Buying Guide: What to Look For

1.  **Corded vs. Cordless:** Cordless is more convenient, but corded models often have stronger suction.
2.  **Wall Climbing:** Only necessary if you have algae problems or a tile waterline.
3.  **Filter Type:** Bag filters are easier to clean than cartridges.

## Conclusion

The **Aiper Scuba S1** offers the best bang-for-buck in 2026. It's cordless, powerful, and climbs walls—features that used to cost $1,000+.
